Legend & Signal Flow Conventions

Carrier Operators - Generate final audio output
Modulator Operators - Control frequency of other operators
Feedback Path - Operator 1 self-modulation
FM Connection - Frequency modulation path

Signal Flow: Operators are numbered 1-6. Arrows show frequency modulation paths where one operator modulates another's frequency. Carriers (red) produce the final audio output, while modulators (blue) shape the harmonic content. Higher numbered operators typically modulate lower numbered ones in chain configurations.

Algorithms 1-8: Complex FM Chains

Long modulation chains for rich, evolving timbres

1
Classic FM Chain
The most complex FM configuration - all 6 operators in series creates maximum harmonic complexity and evolution.

Characteristics:

  • Maximum harmonic complexity
  • Rich, evolving timbres
  • Perfect for brass and complex leads
  • High CPU usage due to serial processing
4
E.PIANO Classic
Two parallel chains create the iconic DX7 electric piano sound. Perfect balance of complexity and clarity.

Characteristics:

  • Iconic electric piano sound
  • Two parallel modulation chains
  • Balanced complexity and clarity
  • Chain 1: Fundamental tone, Chain 2: Bell attack
2
Split Chain
Long chain plus separate carrier. Excellent for bass sounds and split timbres.

Characteristics:

  • Complex chain + simple tone
  • Perfect for bass sounds
  • Two distinct timbres mixed
  • Good for layered sounds
7
Triple Chains
Three separate 2-operator chains create distinct harmonic regions.

Characteristics:

  • Three independent FM pairs
  • Harmonic separation
  • Good for organ-like sounds
  • Clean, defined timbre

Algorithms 9-16: Mixed Configurations

Balanced mix of chains and parallel operators

12
Five Carriers
Near-additive synthesis with 5 carriers and minimal modulation. Perfect for huge lead sounds.

Characteristics:

  • 5 independent carriers
  • Massive, wide sound
  • Perfect for lead synths
  • Additive-like behavior
16
Mixed Parallel
Combination of independent carrier, 2-op chain, and separate modulators.

Characteristics:

  • Three distinct sections
  • Harmonic complexity variety
  • Good for textured sounds
  • Flexible timbral control

Algorithms 17-24: Parallel Operations

Multiple carriers with minimal modulation

25
Pure Additive
All 6 operators as independent carriers - pure additive synthesis for organ tones and harmonic stacks.

Characteristics:

  • All operators are carriers
  • No frequency modulation
  • Pure harmonic addition
  • Perfect for organ sounds
  • Clean, mathematical timbres
32
Parallel Modulation
Two modulators affecting four carriers simultaneously - complex but controlled FM.

Characteristics:

  • Complex parallel modulation
  • Two modulators affect all carriers
  • Rich harmonic interaction
  • Controllable complexity

Algorithm Selection Guide: For classic DX7 sounds, use algorithms 1-8. For modern synthesis and huge sounds, try 12-25. Algorithm 4 is the famous E.PIANO, Algorithm 12 creates massive leads, and Algorithm 25 provides pure additive synthesis. Higher carrier counts generally mean fuller, wider sounds, while longer modulation chains create more complex harmonic evolution.
